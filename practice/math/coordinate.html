Question 1

Easy

Find the distance between points $A(3, 4)$ and $B(7, 1)$

Solution:

Step 1: Distance formula

$d = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}$

Step 2: Identify coordinates

$A(3, 4)$: $x_1 = 3, y_1 = 4$

$B(7, 1)$: $x_2 = 7, y_2 = 1$

Step 3: Substitute values

$d = \sqrt{(7-3)^2 + (1-4)^2}$

$= \sqrt{4^2 + (-3)^2}$

$= \sqrt{16 + 9}$

$= \sqrt{25} = 5$

Answer: 5 units

Question 2

Medium

Find the equation of a line passing through points $(2, 3)$ and $(4, 7)$

Solution:

Step 1: Find slope

$m = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} = \frac{7-3}{4-2} = \frac{4}{2} = 2$

Step 2: Use point-slope form

$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$

Using point $(2, 3)$:

$y - 3 = 2(x - 2)$

Step 3: Simplify

$y - 3 = 2x - 4$

$y = 2x - 4 + 3$

$y = 2x - 1$

Step 4: Standard form (optional)

$2x - y - 1 = 0$

Answer: $y = 2x - 1$ or $2x - y - 1 = 0$

Question 3

Easy

Find the coordinates of the midpoint of the line segment joining $A(2, 5)$ and $B(8, 11)$

Solution:

Step 1: Midpoint formula

$M = \left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)$

Step 2: Substitute coordinates

$M = \left(\frac{2+8}{2}, \frac{5+11}{2}\right)$

Step 3: Calculate

$M = \left(\frac{10}{2}, \frac{16}{2}\right)$

$M = (5, 8)$

Answer: $(5, 8)$

Question 4

Hard

Find the equation of the circle with center $(3, -2)$ and radius $5$ units

Solution:

Step 1: Standard form of circle

$(x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2$

Where $(h, k)$ is center and $r$ is radius

Step 2: Identify values

Center: $(h, k) = (3, -2)$

Radius: $r = 5$

Step 3: Substitute

$(x-3)^2 + (y-(-2))^2 = 5^2$

$(x-3)^2 + (y+2)^2 = 25$

Step 4: Expanded form (optional)

$x^2 - 6x + 9 + y^2 + 4y + 4 = 25$

$x^2 + y^2 - 6x + 4y - 12 = 0$

Answer: $(x-3)^2 + (y+2)^2 = 25$

Question 5

Medium

Find the area of triangle with vertices $A(1, 2)$, $B(4, 6)$, and $C(5, 1)$

Solution:

Step 1: Formula for area of triangle

$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}|x_1(y_2-y_3) + x_2(y_3-y_1) + x_3(y_1-y_2)|$

Step 2: Identify coordinates

$A(1, 2)$: $x_1=1, y_1=2$

$B(4, 6)$: $x_2=4, y_2=6$

$C(5, 1)$: $x_3=5, y_3=1$

Step 3: Substitute values

$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}|1(6-1) + 4(1-2) + 5(2-6)|$

$= \frac{1}{2}|1(5) + 4(-1) + 5(-4)|$

$= \frac{1}{2}|5 - 4 - 20|$

$= \frac{1}{2}|-19|$

$= \frac{19}{2} = 9.5$ square units

Answer: $\frac{19}{2}$ or $9.5$ square units

Question 6

Hard

Find the equation of the parabola with vertex at origin and focus at $(0, 3)$

Solution:

Step 1: Identify parabola orientation

Vertex at origin: $(0, 0)$

Focus at $(0, 3)$: parabola opens upward (along y-axis)

Step 2: Standard form for vertical parabola

$x^2 = 4ay$

Where $a$ is distance from vertex to focus

Step 3: Find value of a

Focus is at $(0, a)$

Given focus: $(0, 3)$

Therefore, $a = 3$

Step 4: Write equation

$x^2 = 4(3)y$

$x^2 = 12y$

Answer: $x^2 = 12y$

Question 7

Medium

Find the slope of the line perpendicular to the line $3x + 4y = 12$

Solution:

Step 1: Convert to slope-intercept form

$3x + 4y = 12$

$4y = -3x + 12$

$y = -\frac{3}{4}x + 3$

Step 2: Identify slope

Slope of given line: $m_1 = -\frac{3}{4}$

Step 3: Use perpendicular slope property

If two lines are perpendicular: $m_1 \times m_2 = -1$

$-\frac{3}{4} \times m_2 = -1$

$m_2 = \frac{-1}{-3/4} = \frac{4}{3}$

Answer: $\frac{4}{3}$

Question 8

Hard

Find the equation of the ellipse with foci at $(±3, 0)$ and vertices at $(±5, 0)$

Solution:

Step 1: Identify ellipse orientation

Foci and vertices on x-axis → horizontal ellipse

Standard form: $\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$

Step 2: Find values

Vertices at $(±a, 0)$ → $a = 5$

Foci at $(±c, 0)$ → $c = 3$

Step 3: Use relationship $c^2 = a^2 - b^2$

$3^2 = 5^2 - b^2$

$9 = 25 - b^2$

$b^2 = 16$

$b = 4$

Step 4: Write equation

$\frac{x^2}{25} + \frac{y^2}{16} = 1$

Answer: $\frac{x^2}{25} + \frac{y^2}{16} = 1$
