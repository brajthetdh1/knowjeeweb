ELECTROSTATICS PROBLEM

Three charges +Q, -2Q, and +Q are placed at vertices of an equilateral triangle of side 'a'. Find the electric field and potential at the centroid of the triangle.

Given Values

• Charge 1: +Q

• Charge 2: -2Q

• Charge 3: +Q

• Side length: a

• Equilateral triangle

Required

• Electric field at centroid

• Electric potential at centroid

• Direction of net field

This problem tests vector addition of electric fields and scalar addition of potentials

Understanding the Geometry

Triangle Configuration

We have an equilateral triangle with:

Vertex A: +Q

Vertex B: -2Q

Vertex C: +Q

The centroid is equidistant from all vertices.

Key Distances

For an equilateral triangle of side 'a':

Distance from vertex to centroid, r = a/√3

This comes from geometry: centroid divides median in 2:1 ratio

Symmetry Analysis

The system has symmetry about the line through vertex B (-2Q) and centroid.

The two +Q charges are symmetric with respect to this line.

Electric Field - Vector Quantity

Electric Field Formula

E = kQ/r² (magnitude)

Direction: Away from positive charges, toward negative charges

Since electric field is a vector, we need to add them vectorially.

Field Directions at Centroid

+Q at A: Field points AWAY from A
-2Q at B: Field points TOWARD B
+Q at C: Field points AWAY from C

Symmetry Advantage

The fields from the two +Q charges (A and C) are symmetric.

Their horizontal components cancel out, leaving only vertical components.

Step 1: Calculate Electric Field

Distance Calculation

All charges are at equal distance from centroid:

r = a/√3

Field Magnitudes

E₁ = kQ/r² (from +Q at A)

E₂ = k(2Q)/r² (from -2Q at B)

E₃ = kQ/r² (from +Q at C)

Note: E₂ is twice as large as E₁ and E₃

Vector Components

Due to symmetry, fields from A and C cancel horizontally.

Let's consider angles: In equilateral triangle, angle between lines from centroid to vertices is 120°.

Step 2: Vector Addition of Fields

Coordinate System

Place centroid at origin, vertex B (-2Q) on positive y-axis.

Then vertices A and C are symmetric about y-axis.

Field from -2Q at B

Since it's negative charge, field points toward it (negative y-direction):

E₂ = - (2kQ/r²) ĵ

Fields from +Q charges

Due to symmetry, x-components cancel, y-components add:

E₁ + E₃ = 2 × (kQ/r²) × cos30° ĵ

= 2 × (kQ/r²) × (√3/2) ĵ = (√3 kQ/r²) ĵ

Step 3: Net Electric Field

Total Field Calculation

Net electric field E_net = E₁ + E₂ + E₃

E_net = [√3 kQ/r² - 2kQ/r²] ĵ

= kQ/r² (√3 - 2) ĵ

Substitute r = a/√3

E_net = kQ/(a²/3) × (√3 - 2) ĵ

= (3kQ/a²)(√3 - 2) ĵ

Direction Analysis

Since √3 ≈ 1.732, then (√3 - 2) ≈ -0.268 (negative)

Therefore, net field is in negative y-direction (toward the -2Q charge).

Step 4: Electric Potential at Centroid

Potential Formula

V = kQ/r (scalar)

Electric potential is a scalar quantity - we simply add algebraically.

Potential from Each Charge

V₁ = k(+Q)/r = kQ/r

V₂ = k(-2Q)/r = -2kQ/r

V₃ = k(+Q)/r = kQ/r

Total Potential

V_total = V₁ + V₂ + V₃

= kQ/r - 2kQ/r + kQ/r = 0

✓ Potential at centroid is ZERO

Final Results & Verification

FINAL ANSWERS

Electric Field

E = (3kQ/a²)(√3 - 2) ĵ

Direction: Toward -2Q charge

Electric Potential

V = 0

Scalar sum cancels out

Physical Interpretation

• Electric field is NON-ZERO: There is force on test charge

• Electric potential is ZERO: Work done to bring test charge from infinity is zero

This is a common scenario in electrostatics - zero potential doesn't mean zero field!

JEE Strategy

• Remember: E is vector (add vectorially), V is scalar (add algebraically)

• Use symmetry to simplify calculations

• Always check if result makes physical sense

Complete Solution Summary

Key Steps Recap

1. Geometry: r = a/√3 from centroid to vertices

2. Field Magnitudes: E = kQ/r² for each charge

3. Vector Addition: Use symmetry, cancel x-components

4. Net Field: E_net = (3kQ/a²)(√3 - 2) ĵ

5. Potential: V_total = kQ/r - 2kQ/r + kQ/r = 0

Important Concepts

• Electric Field: Vector, follows superposition principle vectorially

• Electric Potential: Scalar, follows superposition principle algebraically

• Symmetry: Powerful tool to simplify problems

• Distance: In equilateral triangle, centroid is equidistant from vertices
