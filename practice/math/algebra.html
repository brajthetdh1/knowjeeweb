Question 1

Easy

Solve the quadratic equation:

$x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$

Solution:

Step 1: Factorize the equation

$x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$

Find two numbers that multiply to give 6 and add to give -5

These numbers are -2 and -3

$(x - 2)(x - 3) = 0$

Step 2: Apply zero product property

Either $(x - 2) = 0$ or $(x - 3) = 0$

Step 3: Solve for x

$x - 2 = 0 \Rightarrow x = 2$

$x - 3 = 0 \Rightarrow x = 3$

Answer: $x = 2$ or $x = 3$

Question 2

Medium

If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the roots of equation $2x^2 - 5x + 3 = 0$, find the value of $\alpha^2 + \beta^2$

Solution:

Step 1: Find sum and product of roots

For equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

Sum of roots: $\alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} = -\frac{(-5)}{2} = \frac{5}{2}$

Product of roots: $\alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{3}{2}$

Step 2: Use the identity

$\alpha^2 + \beta^2 = (\alpha + \beta)^2 - 2\alpha\beta$

Step 3: Substitute values

$\alpha^2 + \beta^2 = \left(\frac{5}{2}\right)^2 - 2 \times \frac{3}{2}$

$= \frac{25}{4} - 3$

$= \frac{25}{4} - \frac{12}{4}$

$= \frac{13}{4}$

Answer: $\alpha^2 + \beta^2 = \frac{13}{4}$

Question 3

Easy

Find the value of $x$ if:

$3x + 7 = 22$

Solution:

Step 1: Subtract 7 from both sides

$3x + 7 - 7 = 22 - 7$

$3x = 15$

Step 2: Divide both sides by 3

$\frac{3x}{3} = \frac{15}{3}$

$x = 5$

Step 3: Verify the answer

Substitute $x = 5$ in original equation:

$3(5) + 7 = 15 + 7 = 22$ ✓

Answer: $x = 5$

Question 4

Hard

Solve for $x$:

$\sqrt{x + 3} + \sqrt{x - 2} = 5$

Solution:

Step 1: Isolate one radical

$\sqrt{x + 3} = 5 - \sqrt{x - 2}$

Step 2: Square both sides

$x + 3 = 25 - 10\sqrt{x - 2} + (x - 2)$

$x + 3 = 23 + x - 10\sqrt{x - 2}$

$10\sqrt{x - 2} = 20$

$\sqrt{x - 2} = 2$

Step 3: Square again

$x - 2 = 4$

$x = 6$

Step 4: Verify the solution

$\sqrt{6 + 3} + \sqrt{6 - 2} = \sqrt{9} + \sqrt{4} = 3 + 2 = 5$ ✓

Answer: $x = 6$

Question 5

Medium

Find the coefficient of $x^5$ in the expansion of $(2x - 3)^7$

Solution:

Step 1: Use Binomial Theorem

General term: $T_{r+1} = \binom{n}{r} a^{n-r} b^r$

Here, $n = 7$, $a = 2x$, $b = -3$

Step 2: Find which term contains $x^5$

We need $(2x)^{n-r} = (2x)^5$

So, $n - r = 5$

$7 - r = 5$

$r = 2$

Step 3: Calculate the term

$T_3 = \binom{7}{2} (2x)^5 (-3)^2$

$= \frac{7!}{2!5!} \times 32x^5 \times 9$

$= 21 \times 32 \times 9 \times x^5$

$= 6048x^5$

Answer: Coefficient of $x^5$ is $6048$

Question 6

Hard

If one root of the equation $x^2 + px + q = 0$ is the square of the other, prove that:

$p^3 + q^2 + q = 3pq$

Solution:

Step 1: Define the roots

Let the roots be $\alpha$ and $\alpha^2$

Step 2: Write sum and product

Sum of roots: $\alpha + \alpha^2 = -p$

Product of roots: $\alpha \times \alpha^2 = \alpha^3 = q$

Step 3: From product equation

$\alpha^3 = q$

So, $\alpha = q^{1/3}$

Step 4: Substitute in sum equation

$q^{1/3} + q^{2/3} = -p$

Cubing both sides:

$(q^{1/3} + q^{2/3})^3 = -p^3$

$q + 3q^{1/3} \cdot q^{2/3}(q^{1/3} + q^{2/3}) + q^2 = -p^3$

$q + 3q(-p) + q^2 = -p^3$

$q - 3pq + q^2 = -p^3$

Step 5: Rearrange

$p^3 + q^2 + q = 3pq$

Hence Proved: $p^3 + q^2 + q = 3pq$
