Question 1

JEE Main 2024
Previous Year

A particle is moving in a circular path of radius $r$. The displacement after half a circle would be:

(A) Zero

(B) $\pi r$

(C) $2r$

(D) $2\pi r$

Solution:

Step 1: Understand displacement vs distance

Displacement = Shortest distance between initial and final positions (straight line)

Distance = Total path length traveled

Step 2: Visualize the motion

After half circle, particle moves from one end of diameter to the opposite end

Initial position: Point A

Final position: Point B (diametrically opposite)

Step 3: Calculate displacement

Displacement = Straight line distance from A to B

This is the diameter of the circle = $2r$

Note:

Distance traveled = $\pi r$ (half the circumference)

But displacement = $2r$ (diameter)

Answer: (C) $2r$

Question 2

JEE Advanced 2023
Previous Year

A constant force of 10 N acts on a body of mass 5 kg for 4 seconds. Find the change in momentum of the body.

Solution:

Step 1: Apply Newton's Second Law in terms of momentum

$F = \frac{dp}{dt}$

Where $F$ = force, $p$ = momentum, $t$ = time

Step 2: Rearrange to find change in momentum

$F \cdot dt = dp$

$\Delta p = F \cdot \Delta t$

Step 3: Substitute given values

$F = 10$ N

$\Delta t = 4$ s

$\Delta p = 10 \times 4 = 40$ kg·m/s

Verification using acceleration:

$a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{10}{5} = 2$ m/s²

$v = u + at = 0 + 2(4) = 8$ m/s (assuming initially at rest)

$\Delta p = m \times \Delta v = 5 \times 8 = 40$ kg·m/s ✓

Answer: 40 kg·m/s

Question 3

JEE Main 2023
Previous Year

Two resistors of resistance $R_1 = 100 \Omega$ and $R_2 = 200 \Omega$ are connected in parallel. The equivalent resistance is:

Solution:

Step 1: Formula for parallel resistance

For resistors in parallel:

$\frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2}$

Step 2: Substitute values

$\frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \frac{1}{100} + \frac{1}{200}$

Step 3: Find common denominator

$\frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \frac{2}{200} + \frac{1}{200} = \frac{3}{200}$

Step 4: Take reciprocal

$R_{eq} = \frac{200}{3} = 66.67 \Omega$

Alternative formula:

$R_{eq} = \frac{R_1 \times R_2}{R_1 + R_2} = \frac{100 \times 200}{100 + 200} = \frac{20000}{300} = 66.67 \Omega$

Answer: $66.67 \Omega$ or $\frac{200}{3} \Omega$

Question 4

JEE Advanced 2022
Previous Year

A convex lens of focal length 20 cm forms an image at a distance of 30 cm from the lens. Find the object distance.

Solution:

Step 1: Write the lens formula

$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u}$

Where: $f$ = focal length, $v$ = image distance, $u$ = object distance

Step 2: Identify given values with sign convention

$f = +20$ cm (convex lens, positive)

$v = +30$ cm (real image, positive)

$u = ?$ (to be found, will be negative)

Step 3: Substitute in lens formula

$\frac{1}{20} = \frac{1}{30} - \frac{1}{u}$

Step 4: Solve for u

$\frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{30} - \frac{1}{20}$

$\frac{1}{u} = \frac{2 - 3}{60} = \frac{-1}{60}$

$u = -60$ cm

Step 5: Interpret the result

Negative sign indicates object is on the left side of lens (real object)

Object distance = 60 cm from the lens

Answer: Object distance = 60 cm

Question 5

JEE Main 2022
Previous Year

The work function of a metal is 4 eV. If light of wavelength 3000 Å is incident on it, calculate the maximum kinetic energy of emitted photoelectrons. (Given: $h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34}$ J·s, $c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s, 1 eV = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ J)

Solution:

Step 1: Einstein's photoelectric equation

$E = W_0 + KE_{max}$

$hf = W_0 + KE_{max}$

$KE_{max} = hf - W_0$

Step 2: Calculate energy of incident photon

$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$

$\lambda = 3000$ Å $= 3000 \times 10^{-10}$ m $= 3 \times 10^{-7}$ m

$E = \frac{6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^8}{3 \times 10^{-7}}$

$E = 6.63 \times 10^{-19}$ J

Step 3: Convert to eV

$E = \frac{6.63 \times 10^{-19}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} = 4.14$ eV

Step 4: Calculate maximum kinetic energy

$KE_{max} = E - W_0$

$KE_{max} = 4.14 - 4.0 = 0.14$ eV

Step 5: Convert to Joules (if needed)

$KE_{max} = 0.14 \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19} = 2.24 \times 10^{-20}$ J

Answer: $KE_{max} = 0.14$ eV or $2.24 \times 10^{-20}$ J

Question 6

JEE Main 2021
Previous Year

An ideal gas undergoes an isothermal process. If its volume is doubled, what happens to its pressure?

Solution:

Step 1: Understand isothermal process

Isothermal process: Temperature remains constant

For ideal gas: $PV = nRT$

Step 2: Apply condition for isothermal process

Since $T$ is constant and $n, R$ are constants:

$PV = \text{constant}$

$P_1V_1 = P_2V_2$

Step 3: Apply the given condition

Given: $V_2 = 2V_1$ (volume is doubled)

$P_1V_1 = P_2(2V_1)$

$P_1 = 2P_2$

$P_2 = \frac{P_1}{2}$

Step 4: Interpret the result

The pressure becomes half of its initial value

This follows Boyle's Law: $P \propto \frac{1}{V}$ at constant temperature

Answer: Pressure becomes half (reduces to 50%)
