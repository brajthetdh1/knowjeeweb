Question 1

Easy

Evaluate the limit:

$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{3x}$

Solution:

Step 1: Recall standard limit

$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1$

Step 2: Manipulate to standard form

$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{3x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{5x} \times \frac{5x}{3x}$

$= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{5x} \times \frac{5}{3}$

Step 3: Apply limit

Let $t = 5x$, when $x \to 0$, then $t \to 0$

$= \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\sin t}{t} \times \frac{5}{3}$

$= 1 \times \frac{5}{3} = \frac{5}{3}$

Answer: $\frac{5}{3}$

Question 2

Medium

Find $\frac{dy}{dx}$ if $y = x^x$

Solution:

Step 1: Take logarithm on both sides

$y = x^x$

$\ln y = \ln(x^x)$

$\ln y = x \ln x$

Step 2: Differentiate using implicit differentiation

$\frac{d}{dx}(\ln y) = \frac{d}{dx}(x \ln x)$

$\frac{1}{y} \cdot \frac{dy}{dx} = \ln x + x \cdot \frac{1}{x}$

$\frac{1}{y} \cdot \frac{dy}{dx} = \ln x + 1$

Step 3: Solve for dy/dx

$\frac{dy}{dx} = y(\ln x + 1)$

$\frac{dy}{dx} = x^x(\ln x + 1)$

Answer: $\frac{dy}{dx} = x^x(\ln x + 1)$

Question 3

Easy

Evaluate the integral:

$\int x^2 dx$

Solution:

Step 1: Use power rule for integration

$\int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C$ where $n \neq -1$

Step 2: Apply the formula

Here, $n = 2$

$\int x^2 dx = \frac{x^{2+1}}{2+1} + C$

$= \frac{x^3}{3} + C$

Step 3: Verify by differentiation

$\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{x^3}{3} + C\right) = \frac{3x^2}{3} = x^2$ ✓

Answer: $\frac{x^3}{3} + C$

Question 4

Hard

Evaluate using L'Hôpital's Rule:

$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^x - 1 - x}{x^2}$

Solution:

Step 1: Check indeterminate form

At $x = 0$: $\frac{e^0 - 1 - 0}{0^2} = \frac{0}{0}$ (indeterminate)

Step 2: Apply L'Hôpital's Rule (1st time)

$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^x - 1 - x}{x^2} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^x - 1}{2x}$

Step 3: Check again

At $x = 0$: $\frac{e^0 - 1}{0} = \frac{0}{0}$ (still indeterminate)

Step 4: Apply L'Hôpital's Rule (2nd time)

$= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^x}{2}$

$= \frac{e^0}{2} = \frac{1}{2}$

Answer: $\frac{1}{2}$

Question 5

Medium

Find the area bounded by the curve $y = x^2$, x-axis, and the lines $x = 0$ and $x = 2$

Solution:

Step 1: Set up the definite integral

Area = $\int_0^2 x^2 dx$

Step 2: Find the antiderivative

$\int x^2 dx = \frac{x^3}{3}$

Step 3: Apply limits

Area = $\left[\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^2$

$= \frac{2^3}{3} - \frac{0^3}{3}$

$= \frac{8}{3} - 0$

$= \frac{8}{3}$ square units

Answer: $\frac{8}{3}$ square units

Question 6

Hard

Evaluate the integral using integration by parts:

$\int x e^x dx$

Solution:

Step 1: Formula for integration by parts

$\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$

Step 2: Choose u and dv

Let $u = x$ and $dv = e^x dx$

Then $du = dx$ and $v = e^x$

Step 3: Apply the formula

$\int x e^x dx = x \cdot e^x - \int e^x dx$

$= xe^x - e^x + C$

$= e^x(x - 1) + C$

Step 4: Verify by differentiation

$\frac{d}{dx}[e^x(x-1)] = e^x(x-1) + e^x = xe^x$ ✓

Answer: $e^x(x - 1) + C$ or $xe^x - e^x + C$

Question 7

Medium

Find the derivative of $y = \sin^{-1}(2x)$

Solution:

Step 1: Formula for derivative of sin⁻¹(x)

$\frac{d}{dx}[\sin^{-1}(x)] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$

Step 2: Apply chain rule

Let $u = 2x$, then $y = \sin^{-1}(u)$

$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$

Step 3: Calculate each part

$\frac{dy}{du} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-u^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-(2x)^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-4x^2}}$

$\frac{du}{dx} = 2$

Step 4: Multiply

$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-4x^2}} \times 2 = \frac{2}{\sqrt{1-4x^2}}$

Answer: $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{1-4x^2}}$

Question 8

Hard

Find the maximum and minimum values of $f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 - 9x + 5$ in the interval $[-2, 4]$

Solution:

Step 1: Find critical points

$f'(x) = 3x^2 - 6x - 9 = 3(x^2 - 2x - 3) = 3(x-3)(x+1)$

Set $f'(x) = 0$: $x = 3$ or $x = -1$

Step 2: Check which critical points are in [-2, 4]

$x = -1$ ✓ (in interval)

$x = 3$ ✓ (in interval)

Step 3: Evaluate at critical points and endpoints

$f(-2) = (-2)^3 - 3(-2)^2 - 9(-2) + 5 = -8 - 12 + 18 + 5 = 3$

$f(-1) = (-1)^3 - 3(-1)^2 - 9(-1) + 5 = -1 - 3 + 9 + 5 = 10$

$f(3) = 3^3 - 3(3)^2 - 9(3) + 5 = 27 - 27 - 27 + 5 = -22$

$f(4) = 4^3 - 3(4)^2 - 9(4) + 5 = 64 - 48 - 36 + 5 = -15$

Step 4: Compare values

Maximum value = 10 (at $x = -1$)

Minimum value = -22 (at $x = 3$)

Answer: Maximum = 10, Minimum = -22
