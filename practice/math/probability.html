Question 1

Medium

A bag contains 5 red balls and 3 blue balls. Two balls are drawn at random without replacement. Find the probability that both balls are of the same color.

Solution:

Step 1: Total ways to draw 2 balls

Total balls = 5 + 3 = 8

$^8C_2 = \frac{8!}{2!6!} = \frac{8 \times 7}{2} = 28$

Step 2: Favorable outcomes (both same color)

Case 1: Both red = $^5C_2 = \frac{5 \times 4}{2} = 10$

Case 2: Both blue = $^3C_2 = \frac{3 \times 2}{2} = 3$

Total favorable = 10 + 3 = 13

Step 3: Calculate probability

$P(\text{same color}) = \frac{13}{28}$

Answer: $\frac{13}{28}$

Question 2

Hard

If $P(A) = 0.6$, $P(B) = 0.5$, and $P(A \cap B) = 0.3$, find:

(i) $P(A|B)$

(ii) $P(A \cup B)$

(iii) $P(A'|B)$

Solution:

Part (i): Find P(A|B)

Conditional probability: $P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$

$P(A|B) = \frac{0.3}{0.5} = 0.6$

Part (ii): Find P(A ∪ B)

$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$

$= 0.6 + 0.5 - 0.3 = 0.8$

Part (iii): Find P(A'|B)

$P(A'|B) = 1 - P(A|B)$

$= 1 - 0.6 = 0.4$

Answer: (i) 0.6, (ii) 0.8, (iii) 0.4

Question 3

Medium

Find the mean and variance of the following data: 5, 8, 10, 12, 15

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate Mean

$\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n} = \frac{5 + 8 + 10 + 12 + 15}{5} = \frac{50}{5} = 10$

Step 2: Calculate deviations and squares

$(x_i - \bar{x})$: -5, -2, 0, 2, 5

$(x_i - \bar{x})^2$: 25, 4, 0, 4, 25

$\sum(x_i - \bar{x})^2 = 58$

Step 3: Calculate Variance

$\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum(x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n} = \frac{58}{5} = 11.6$

Step 4: Standard Deviation (bonus)

$\sigma = \sqrt{11.6} \approx 3.41$

Answer: Mean = 10, Variance = 11.6

Question 4

Hard

A die is thrown 6 times. Find the probability of getting exactly 3 sixes using binomial distribution.

Solution:

Step 1: Identify binomial parameters

$n = 6$ (number of trials)

$r = 3$ (number of successes)

$p = \frac{1}{6}$ (probability of getting a six)

$q = 1 - p = \frac{5}{6}$ (probability of not getting a six)

Step 2: Binomial probability formula

$P(X = r) = ^nC_r \cdot p^r \cdot q^{n-r}$

Step 3: Calculate combination

$^6C_3 = \frac{6!}{3!3!} = \frac{6 \times 5 \times 4}{3 \times 2 \times 1} = 20$

Step 4: Substitute values

$P(X = 3) = 20 \times \left(\frac{1}{6}\right)^3 \times \left(\frac{5}{6}\right)^3$

$= 20 \times \frac{1}{216} \times \frac{125}{216}$

$= \frac{2500}{46656} = \frac{625}{11664}$

Answer: $\frac{625}{11664} \approx 0.0536$ or $5.36\%$

Question 5

Hard

Three machines A, B, and C produce 50%, 30%, and 20% of the total output respectively. The defective rates are 3%, 4%, and 5%. If a randomly selected item is defective, find the probability it was produced by machine A.

Solution:

Step 1: Use Bayes' Theorem

$P(A|D) = \frac{P(D|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(D)}$

Step 2: Identify probabilities

$P(A) = 0.50$, $P(B) = 0.30$, $P(C) = 0.20$

$P(D|A) = 0.03$, $P(D|B) = 0.04$, $P(D|C) = 0.05$

Step 3: Calculate total probability of defective

$P(D) = P(D|A) \cdot P(A) + P(D|B) \cdot P(B) + P(D|C) \cdot P(C)$

$= (0.03)(0.50) + (0.04)(0.30) + (0.05)(0.20)$

$= 0.015 + 0.012 + 0.010 = 0.037$

Step 4: Apply Bayes' theorem

$P(A|D) = \frac{0.03 \times 0.50}{0.037} = \frac{0.015}{0.037} = \frac{15}{37}$

Answer: $\frac{15}{37} \approx 0.405$ or $40.5\%$

Question 7

Hard

A random variable X has the following probability distribution:

X 0 1 2 3
P(X) 0.1 0.3 0.4 0.2

Find E(X) and Var(X)

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate E(X)

$E(X) = \sum x_i \cdot P(x_i)$

$= 0(0.1) + 1(0.3) + 2(0.4) + 3(0.2)$

$= 0 + 0.3 + 0.8 + 0.6 = 1.7$

Step 2: Calculate E(X²)

$E(X^2) = \sum x_i^2 \cdot P(x_i)$

$= 0^2(0.1) + 1^2(0.3) + 2^2(0.4) + 3^2(0.2)$

$= 0 + 0.3 + 1.6 + 1.8 = 3.7$

Step 3: Calculate Var(X)

$Var(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2$

$= 3.7 - (1.7)^2$

$= 3.7 - 2.89 = 0.81$

Answer: E(X) = 1.7, Var(X) = 0.81

Question 8

Hard

From a deck of 52 cards, 4 cards are drawn. Find the probability that:

(i) All are aces

(ii) All are from the same suit

Solution:

Total ways to draw 4 cards

$^{52}C_4 = \frac{52!}{4!48!} = \frac{52 \times 51 \times 50 \times 49}{4 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1} = 270725$

Part (i): All are aces

There are 4 aces in a deck

Ways to choose all 4 aces = $^4C_4 = 1$

$P(\text{all aces}) = \frac{1}{270725}$

Part (ii): All from same suit

Each suit has 13 cards

Ways to choose 4 cards from one suit = $^{13}C_4 = 715$

Number of suits = 4

Total favorable outcomes = $4 \times 715 = 2860$

$P(\text{same suit}) = \frac{2860}{270725} = \frac{44}{4165}$

Answer: (i) $\frac{1}{270725}$, (ii) $\frac{44}{4165} \approx 0.0106$
