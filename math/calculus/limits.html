Step 1: Concept of Limit

Consider a function

\( f(x) \)

defined near \( x = a \) (except possibly at \( a \) itself).

We want to understand what value \( f(x) \) approaches as \( x \) gets closer and closer to \( a \).

Step 2: Informal Definition

The limit of \( f(x) \) as \( x \to a \) is \( L \) if:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \)

meaning \( f(x) \) can be made arbitrarily close to \( L \) by taking \( x \) sufficiently close to \( a \) (but \( x \neq a \)).

Step 3: Left-hand and Right-hand Limits

Left-hand limit:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) = L \)

Right-hand limit:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = L \)

The limit exists if:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = L \)

Step 4: Rigorous ε-δ Definition

The limit of \( f(x) \) as \( x \to a \) is \( L \) means:

$$ \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \iff \forall \varepsilon > 0, \; \exists \delta > 0 :\\ 0 < |x - a| < \delta \implies |f(x) - L| < \varepsilon $$

In the rigorous ε-δ definition of a limit: ε (epsilon) represents how close we want f(x) to be to L, and δ (delta) represents how close x should be to a (excluding a itself). No matter how small ε is, we can always find a δ that ensures f(x) stays within ε of L.

Step 5: Important Notes

1. Function need not be defined at \( x = a \).

2. Limits can be finite or infinite.

3. One-sided limits are useful for studying discontinuities.

4. JEE problems often involve algebraic manipulation to evaluate limits.

Here are some examples

Step 1: Simple Limit Example

Evaluate: \( \lim_{x \to 2} (3x + 1) \)

\( \lim_{x \to 2} (3x + 1) = ? \)

Solution: Substitute \( x = 2 \)

\( 3(2) + 1 = 7 \)

Answer: 7

Step 2: Limit Leading to 0/0

Evaluate: \( \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2} \)

\( \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2} \)

Solution: Factor numerator:

\( x^2 - 4 = (x-2)(x+2) \)

Cancel (x-2):

\( \lim_{x \to 2} (x + 2) = 4 \)

Answer: 4

Step 3: One-Sided Limits

Function:

\( f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 & x < 1 \\ 2x + 1 & x \ge 1 \end{cases} \)

Left-hand limit: \( \lim_{x \to 1^-} f(x) = 1^2 = 1 \)

Right-hand limit: \( \lim_{x \to 1^+} f(x) = 2(1) + 1 = 3 \)

Since LHL ≠ RHL, overall limit does not exist.

Step 4: Limit at Infinity

Evaluate: \( \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2 + 5}{2x^2 - x} \)

Divide numerator & denominator by \( x^2 \):

\( \frac{3 + 5/x^2}{2 - 1/x} \to \frac{3}{2} \)

Answer: 3/2

Step 5: Standard Tricky Limit

Evaluate: \( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} \)

Answer: 1 (well-known JEE standard limit)

Standard Limits in Calculus

Step 1: Definition of a Limit

The limit of a function \( f(x) \) as \( x \to a \) is the value that \( f(x) \) approaches as \( x \) gets arbitrarily close to \( a \) (but \( x \neq a \)).

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \)

Step 2: Some Standard Limits

  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 \)
  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0 \)
  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x^2} = \frac{1}{2} \)
  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan x}{x} = 1 \)
  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} (1 + x)^{1/x} = e \)
  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{1}{x}\right)^x = e \)

Step 3: Examples

Example 1: \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{x} \)

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} 5 \cdot \frac{\sin 5x}{5x} = 5 \)

Example 2: \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos 3x}{x^2} \)

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos 3x}{x^2} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{9(1 - \cos 3x)}{(3x)^2} = \frac{9}{2} \)

Step 4: Important Notes

  • Always look for standard limits before applying L'Hôpital's Rule.
  • Multiplying and dividing by constants can simplify limits.
  • Many JEE questions combine these limits with algebraic manipulation.
  • Trigonometric limits often require using small angle approximations: \( \sin x \approx x, \tan x \approx x, \cos x \approx 1 - \frac{x^2}{2} \) as \( x \to 0 \).

Techniques to Evaluate Limits

Step 1: Direct Substitution

If the function is continuous at the point, directly substitute the value of \( x \) to find the limit.

Example:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2} (3x^2 + 5) = 3(2)^2 + 5 = 17 \)

Step 2: Factorization

If direct substitution gives an indeterminate form like \( \frac{0}{0} \), try factoring numerator and denominator.

Example:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3} = \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{x-3} = \lim_{x \to 3} (x+3) = 6 \)

Step 3: Rationalization

Multiply by the conjugate if the limit involves square roots to remove indeterminate forms.

Example:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x-4} = \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{x}-2}{x-4} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x}+2}{\sqrt{x}+2} = \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{x-4}{(x-4)(\sqrt{x}+2)} = \frac{1}{4} \)

Step 4: Using Standard Limits

Apply well-known standard limits such as:

  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 \)
  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} (1+x)^{1/x} = e \)
  • \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x^2} = \frac{1}{2} \)

Step 5: L'Hôpital's Rule

For indeterminate forms like \( \frac{0}{0} \) or \( \frac{\infty}{\infty} \), take derivatives of numerator and denominator:

Example:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\cos x}{1} = 1 \)

Step 6: Algebraic Manipulation

Sometimes rearranging terms or splitting fractions helps simplify limits.

Example:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x^2 + 3x}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} (x + 3) = 3 \)

Step 7: Important Notes

  • Always check if the limit can be evaluated directly before using advanced methods.
  • Factorization and rationalization are the most commonly used techniques in JEE problems.
  • Keep standard limits handy—they save a lot of computation time.
  • Check for one-sided limits in case of piecewise functions.
