Question 1

Let $f(x) = x^3 - 3x + 1$. If the equation $f(x) = 0$ has three real roots $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$, then find the value of $\alpha^3 + \beta^3 + \gamma^3$

Solution:

Step 1: Understand the given equation

$f(x) = x^3 - 3x + 1 = 0$

Roots are $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$

Step 2: Since roots satisfy the equation

$\alpha^3 - 3\alpha + 1 = 0 \Rightarrow \alpha^3 = 3\alpha - 1$

$\beta^3 - 3\beta + 1 = 0 \Rightarrow \beta^3 = 3\beta - 1$

$\gamma^3 - 3\gamma + 1 = 0 \Rightarrow \gamma^3 = 3\gamma - 1$

Step 3: Add all three equations

$\alpha^3 + \beta^3 + \gamma^3 = 3(\alpha + \beta + \gamma) - 3$

Step 4: Find sum of roots using Vieta's formulas

For $x^3 + 0x^2 - 3x + 1 = 0$

$\alpha + \beta + \gamma = -\frac{\text{coefficient of } x^2}{\text{coefficient of } x^3} = 0$

Step 5: Calculate final answer

$\alpha^3 + \beta^3 + \gamma^3 = 3(0) - 3 = -3$

Answer: $-3$

Question 2

Previous Year

The number of points in the interval $[-\sqrt{13}, \sqrt{13}]$ at which $f(x) = \sin(x^2)$ attains its maximum value is:

Solution:

Step 1: Find when sin function has maximum

$\sin(x^2)$ has maximum value 1 when $x^2 = \frac{\pi}{2} + 2n\pi$ where $n \in \mathbb{Z}$

$x^2 = (4n+1)\frac{\pi}{2}$

Step 2: Find the constraint

We need $-\sqrt{13} \le x \le \sqrt{13}$

So, $0 \le x^2 \le 13$

Step 3: Find valid values of n

$(4n+1)\frac{\pi}{2} \le 13$

$4n+1 \le \frac{26}{\pi}$

$4n+1 \le 8.28$

$n \le 1.82$

Step 4: Count valid values

$n = 0$: $x^2 = \frac{\pi}{2} \approx 1.57$ → $x = \pm\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2}}$ (2 points)

$n = 1$: $x^2 = \frac{5\pi}{2} \approx 7.85$ → $x = \pm\sqrt{\frac{5\pi}{2}}$ (2 points)

Total = 4 points

Answer: 4 points

Question 3

JEE Main 2023
Previous Year

If $\int \frac{dx}{(x^2+4)(x^2+9)} = A\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + B\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{3}\right) + C$, then find $A$ and $B$.

Solution:

Step 1: Use partial fractions

$\frac{1}{(x^2+4)(x^2+9)} = \frac{Mx+N}{x^2+4} + \frac{Px+Q}{x^2+9}$

Step 2: Since denominator has no linear factors

$\frac{1}{(x^2+4)(x^2+9)} = \frac{M}{x^2+4} + \frac{N}{x^2+9}$

$1 = M(x^2+9) + N(x^2+4)$

Step 3: Solve for M and N

Comparing coefficients:

$x^2$: $M + N = 0$

Constant: $9M + 4N = 1$

From first: $N = -M$

$9M - 4M = 1 \Rightarrow M = \frac{1}{5}$, $N = -\frac{1}{5}$

Step 4: Integrate

$\int \frac{dx}{(x^2+4)(x^2+9)} = \frac{1}{5}\int\frac{dx}{x^2+4} - \frac{1}{5}\int\frac{dx}{x^2+9}$

$= \frac{1}{5} \cdot \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) - \frac{1}{5} \cdot \frac{1}{3}\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{3}\right) + C$

$= \frac{1}{10}\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) - \frac{1}{15}\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{3}\right) + C$

Answer: $A = \frac{1}{10}$, $B = -\frac{1}{15}$

Question 4

The number of $2 \times 2$ matrices $A$ with entries from the set $\{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}$ such that the sum of the diagonal elements of $A^T A$ is 5, where $A^T$ denotes the transpose of $A$, is:

Solution:

Step 1: Set up the matrix

Let $A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}$ where $a,b,c,d \in \{0,1,2,3,4,5\}$

Step 2: Calculate $A^T A$

$A^T = \begin{bmatrix} a & c \\ b & d \end{bmatrix}$

$A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} a & c \\ b & d \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a^2+c^2 & ab+cd \\ ab+cd & b^2+d^2 \end{bmatrix}$

Step 3: Find condition

Sum of diagonal elements = $a^2 + c^2 + b^2 + d^2 = 5$

We need: $a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + d^2 = 5$

Step 4: Find all possible combinations

Since each variable is in $\{0,1,2,3,4,5\}$ and sum of squares = 5:

  • $(2,1,0,0)$ and permutations: $\frac{4!}{2!} = 12$ ways
  • $(1,1,1,\sqrt{2})$ - Not valid (√2 not in set)
  • $(2,1,0,0)$ permutations give us all valid cases

Total permutations of $(2,1,0,0)$ = 12

Answer: 12 matrices

Question 5

The coefficient of $x^{50}$ in the polynomial $(x+1)(x+2)(x+3)...(x+100)$ is:

Solution:

Step 1: Understand the structure

$(x+1)(x+2)(x+3)...(x+100)$ is a polynomial of degree 100

Step 2: Use Vieta's formulas

The coefficient of $x^{50}$ comes from choosing 50 factors with $x$ and 50 constants

This equals the sum of products of 50 numbers taken from $\{1,2,3,...,100\}$

Step 3: Apply symmetric function formula

Coefficient of $x^{50}$ = $e_{50}$ (50th elementary symmetric polynomial)

This is $\binom{100}{50}$ times the sum of all products of 50 numbers

Step 4: Alternative approach - Vieta's for roots

Roots are $-1, -2, -3, ..., -100$

Coefficient of $x^{50}$ = $(-1)^{50} \times e_{50}(1,2,3,...,100)$

This is a very large number requiring computational methods

Answer: $\sum$ (all products of 50 numbers from {1,2,...,100})

Note: Exact numerical answer requires computation

Question 6

JEE Main 2021
Previous Year

If $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(x^2)}{x^2} \cdot \frac{x}{\sin x} \cdot \frac{\tan x}{x} = k$, then find the value of $k$.

Solution:

Step 1: Apply standard limits

We know: $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1$

Also: $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan x}{x} = 1$

Step 2: Evaluate each part

$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(x^2)}{x^2} = 1$ (standard limit with $t = x^2$)

$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x}{\sin x} = \frac{1}{\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x}} = \frac{1}{1} = 1$

$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan x}{x} = 1$

Step 3: Multiply all limits

$k = 1 \times 1 \times 1 = 1$

Answer: $k = 1$
