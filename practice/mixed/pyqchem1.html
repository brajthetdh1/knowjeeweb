Question 1

JEE Main 2024
Previous Year

Calculate the pH of a solution containing 0.1 M CH₃COOH and 0.1 M CH₃COONa. (Given: $K_a$ for CH₃COOH = $1.8 \times 10^{-5}$, $\log 1.8 = 0.26$)

Solution:

Step 1: Identify the system

CH₃COOH (weak acid) + CH₃COONa (salt of weak acid) = Buffer solution

Step 2: Use Henderson-Hasselbalch equation

$\text{pH} = \text{pK}_a + \log\frac{[\text{Salt}]}{[\text{Acid}]}$

Step 3: Calculate pK_a

$\text{pK}_a = -\log K_a = -\log(1.8 \times 10^{-5})$

$= -\log 1.8 - \log 10^{-5}$

$= -0.26 + 5 = 4.74$

Step 4: Substitute values

$\text{pH} = 4.74 + \log\frac{0.1}{0.1}$

$= 4.74 + \log 1$

$= 4.74 + 0 = 4.74$

Answer: pH = 4.74

Question 2

JEE Advanced 2023
Previous Year

For the reaction: N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ 2NH₃(g), $K_p = 1.64 \times 10^{-4}$ at 400°C. Find $K_c$ at the same temperature. (R = 0.082 L·atm·K⁻¹·mol⁻¹)

Solution:

Step 1: Relation between K_p and K_c

$K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n}$

Where $\Delta n$ = moles of gaseous products - moles of gaseous reactants

Step 2: Calculate Δn

N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ 2NH₃(g)

$\Delta n = 2 - (1 + 3) = 2 - 4 = -2$

Step 3: Convert temperature to Kelvin

$T = 400°C = 400 + 273 = 673$ K

Step 4: Rearrange formula to find K_c

$K_c = \frac{K_p}{(RT)^{\Delta n}}$

$K_c = K_p(RT)^{-\Delta n}$

$K_c = 1.64 \times 10^{-4} \times (0.082 \times 673)^{-(-2)}$

$K_c = 1.64 \times 10^{-4} \times (55.186)^{2}$

$K_c = 1.64 \times 10^{-4} \times 3045.5$

$K_c = 0.50$

Answer: $K_c = 0.50$ (mol/L)⁻²

Question 3

JEE Main 2023
Previous Year

Arrange the following compounds in increasing order of their boiling points:

(I) CH₃CH₂CH₂CH₃ (Butane)

(II) CH₃CH₂CH₂OH (1-Propanol)

(III) CH₃CH(OH)CH₃ (2-Propanol)

(IV) CH₃OCH₃ (Dimethyl ether)

Solution:

Step 1: Identify intermolecular forces

(I) Butane: Only Van der Waals forces (weakest)

(II) 1-Propanol: Hydrogen bonding (strong)

(III) 2-Propanol: Hydrogen bonding (strong)

(IV) Dimethyl ether: Dipole-dipole interactions (moderate)

Step 2: Compare molecular masses

Butane: C₄H₁₀ = 58 g/mol

1-Propanol: C₃H₈O = 60 g/mol

2-Propanol: C₃H₈O = 60 g/mol

Dimethyl ether: C₂H₆O = 46 g/mol

Step 3: Apply boiling point rules

Hydrogen bonding > Dipole-dipole > Van der Waals

Alcohols have higher BP than ethers and alkanes

Primary alcohols have slightly higher BP than secondary alcohols (more surface area for H-bonding)

Step 4: Arrange in order

Lowest BP: Dimethyl ether (no H-bonding, smallest)

Next: Butane (only Van der Waals)

Next: 2-Propanol (H-bonding, secondary)

Highest BP: 1-Propanol (H-bonding, primary)

Answer: IV < I < III < II

Dimethyl ether < Butane < 2-Propanol < 1-Propanol

Question 4

JEE Advanced 2022
Previous Year

Calculate the oxidation state of chromium in CrO₅.

Solution:

Step 1: Understand the structure of CrO₅

CrO₅ has a unique structure: Cr is bonded to one O² and four peroxy oxygen atoms (O₂²⁻)

Structure: Cr=O with two -O-O- groups

Step 2: Assign oxidation states to oxygen

One double-bonded O: -2

Four peroxy oxygens (two O-O bonds): -1 each

Step 3: Set up equation

Let oxidation state of Cr = x

Overall charge = 0 (neutral molecule)

$x + 1(-2) + 4(-1) = 0$

$x - 2 - 4 = 0$

$x - 6 = 0$

$x = +6$

Step 4: Verify

Oxidation state: Cr(+6) + O(-2) + 4O(-1) = +6 - 2 - 4 = 0 ✓

Answer: Oxidation state of Cr = +6

Question 5

JEE Main 2022
Previous Year

Identify the product 'X' in the following reaction:

CH₃CHO + HCN → X

Solution:

Step 1: Identify the reaction type

Aldehyde + HCN → Cyanohydrin formation

This is a nucleophilic addition reaction

Step 2: Mechanism

HCN dissociates: HCN ⇌ H⁺ + CN⁻

CN⁻ (nucleophile) attacks the carbonyl carbon

C=O becomes C-O⁻

H⁺ adds to O⁻ forming OH

Step 3: Draw the product

CH₃CHO + HCN → CH₃CH(OH)CN

Product: 2-Hydroxypropanenitrile (Lactonitrile)

Step 4: Name the compound

IUPAC name: 2-Hydroxypropanenitrile

Common name: Acetaldehyde cyanohydrin or Lactonitrile

Answer: X = CH₃CH(OH)CN

(2-Hydroxypropanenitrile / Lactonitrile)

Question 6

JEE Main 2021
Previous Year

The molarity of a solution containing 5.3 g of Na₂CO₃ dissolved in 500 mL solution is: (Molar mass of Na₂CO₃ = 106 g/mol)

Solution:

Step 1: Formula for molarity

$\text{Molarity (M)} = \frac{\text{Number of moles of solute}}{\text{Volume of solution in L}}$

Step 2: Calculate number of moles

$\text{Number of moles} = \frac{\text{Given mass}}{\text{Molar mass}}$

$= \frac{5.3}{106}$

$= 0.05$ mol

Step 3: Convert volume to liters

Volume = 500 mL = 0.5 L

Step 4: Calculate molarity

$\text{Molarity} = \frac{0.05}{0.5}$

$= 0.1$ M

Verification:

0.1 M means 0.1 moles per liter

In 0.5 L: 0.1 × 0.5 = 0.05 mol ✓

Mass = 0.05 × 106 = 5.3 g ✓

Answer: Molarity = 0.1 M
