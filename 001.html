What is Momentum?

Momentum is the product of mass and velocity of an object.

It's a vector quantity with both magnitude and direction.

Key Formula:

p = m·v

SI Unit: kg·m/s

JEE Significance: Foundation for collision problems and conservation laws.

Key aspects:

Mass

Velocity

Vector

Types of Momentum

Linear Momentum

✓ p = m·v

✓ For translational motion

✓ Conserved in isolated systems

✓ JEE Main favorite

Angular Momentum

✓ L = r × p

✓ For rotational motion

✓ L = I·ω (rigid body)

✓ JEE Advanced focus

JEE Memory Tip: Linear momentum for straight-line motion, angular for rotation

Impulse-Momentum Theorem

Impulse-Momentum Theorem

J = Δp = F·Δt

Impulse equals change in momentum

Impulse (J)

✓ J = F·Δt

✓ Area under F-t graph

✓ Vector quantity

Applications

✓ Cushioning in collisions

Conservation of Momentum

Fundamental law in physics:

Law of Conservation of Momentum

Σpinitial = Σpfinal

In an isolated system, total momentum remains constant

Conditions

✓ No external force

✓ Net Fext = 0

✓ Isolated system

Applications

✓ Collisions

✓ Explosions

✓ Rocket propulsion

Types of Collisions

Elastic Collision

✓ Momentum conserved

✓ Kinetic energy conserved

✓ e = 1 (coefficient of restitution)

✓ Example: Billiard balls

Inelastic Collision

✓ Momentum conserved

✓ Kinetic energy NOT conserved

✓ 0 ≤ e < 1

✓ Example: Car crash

JEE Strategy: For perfectly inelastic collision, bodies stick together after collision

JEE Example Problem 1

JEE Main 2022 Question:

A 2 kg ball moving at 4 m/s collides head-on with a stationary 3 kg ball. If the collision is perfectly elastic, find their velocities after collision.

Solution:

Step 1: Conservation of Momentum

m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂

2×4 + 3×0 = 2v₁ + 3v₂

8 = 2v₁ + 3v₂ ...(1)

Step 2: Elastic Collision (e=1)

v₂ - v₁ = u₁ - u₂

v₂ - v₁ = 4 - 0 = 4 ...(2)

Step 3: Solve Equations

From (1) and (2):

v₁ = -0.8 m/s, v₂ = 3.2 m/s

JEE Example Problem 2

JEE Advanced 2020 Question:

A bullet of mass 10 g moving horizontally at 400 m/s strikes a wooden block of mass 2 kg suspended by a string. If the bullet embeds in the block, find the height to which the block rises.

Solution:

Step 1: Perfectly Inelastic Collision

m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = (m₁+m₂)v

0.01×400 + 2×0 = (0.01+2)v

4 = 2.01v ⇒ v ≈ 1.99 m/s

Step 2: Energy Conservation

½(m₁+m₂)v² = (m₁+m₂)gh

½×2.01×(1.99)² = 2.01×9.8×h

h ≈ 0.202 m = 20.2 cm
