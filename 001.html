Newton's First Law of Motion

The Law Statement

"Every body continues in its state of rest or of uniform motion in a straight line unless it is compelled to change that state by forces impressed upon it."

Key Points:

• Defines the concept of INERTIA

• Qualitative law (no formula)

• Defines inertial frames of reference

• Foundation for Newton's Second Law

Also known as:

Law of Inertia

First Law

Galileo's Law

What is Inertia?

Definition of Inertia

"The tendency of an object to resist changes in its state of motion"

Mass is the quantitative measure of inertia

Types of Inertia

✓ Inertia of Rest

✓ Inertia of Motion

✓ Inertia of Direction

✓ Rotational Inertia

Mass vs Inertia

• Mass measures inertia

• More mass = More inertia

• Scalar quantity

• SI unit: kilogram (kg)

JEE Memory Tip: Inertia is the 'laziness' of an object - its resistance to change in motion!

Types of Inertia

Inertia of Rest

✓ Tendency to remain at rest

✓ Example: Sudden bus stop

✓ Dust from carpet beating

✓ Coin on card trick

Inertia of Motion

✓ Tendency to remain in motion

✓ Example: Sudden bus start

✓ Athlete running before jump

✓ Seat belts in cars

Inertia of Direction

✓ Tendency to maintain direction

✓ Example: Turning car

✓ Stone from sling

✓ Planetary motion

Rotational Inertia

✓ Resistance to rotational change

✓ Depends on mass distribution

✓ Example: Spinning top

✓ Gyroscope stability

Continues orbital motion due to inertia

Inertial Frames of Reference

Definition

"A frame of reference in which Newton's First Law holds true"

No acceleration, no rotation

Examples of Inertial Frames

• Earth (approximately)

• Laboratory at rest

• Train moving with constant velocity

• Spaceship with constant velocity

Non-Inertial Frames

• Accelerating car

• Rotating platform

• Merry-go-round

• Taking off airplane

JEE Advanced: In non-inertial frames, we introduce pseudo forces to apply Newton's laws!

JEE Example Problem 1

JEE Main 2022 Question:

Why do passengers in a moving bus tend to fall forward when the bus suddenly stops? Explain using Newton's First Law.

Solution:

Step 1: Identify the inertia type

This demonstrates inertia of motion

Step 2: Analyze the situation

• Bus and passengers are moving together

• When bus stops suddenly, lower body stops due to friction

• Upper body continues moving forward due to inertia

Step 3: Newton's First Law application

"Body continues in state of uniform motion unless acted upon by external force"

No external force acts on upper body immediately, so it continues forward motion

JEE Example Problem 2

JEE Advanced 2020 Question:

A coin is placed on a card covering a glass. When the card is flicked quickly, the coin falls into the glass. Explain this using Law of Inertia.

Solution:

Step 1: Identify inertia type

This demonstrates inertia of rest

Step 2: Analyze the forces

• Card receives quick horizontal force

• Coin tends to remain at rest due to inertia

• Very little friction between card and coin

Step 3: Newton's First Law application

"Body continues in state of rest unless acted upon by external force"

Coin remains almost stationary while card moves away, then falls due to gravity

Common Misconceptions

Wrong Concepts

✗ Force is needed to maintain motion

✗ Objects naturally come to rest

✗ Inertia is a force

✗ Heavier objects fall faster

Correct Concepts

✓ Force changes motion

✓ Friction stops objects

✓ Inertia is a property

✓ All objects fall equally

JEE Strategy: Always remember - Inertia is NOT a force! It's the property of matter to resist changes in motion.
