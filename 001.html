Advanced Relative Motion Problem

JEE Main & Advanced Level

Problem Statement

JEE Advanced Level Problem:

A swimmer can swim in still water at 5 m/s. He wants to cross a river of width 120 m flowing at 3 m/s. He starts from point A and wants to reach exactly opposite point B on the other bank.

However, there's a twist: After swimming for 10 seconds, he realizes he's off course and immediately adjusts his swimming direction to reach point B.

Find the total time taken to cross the river and the angle at which he should swim after adjustment.

Understanding the Problem

Key Elements:

  • River width: 120 m
  • River speed: 3 m/s
  • Swimmer speed: 5 m/s
  • Initial swimming time: 10 s
  • Target: Point exactly opposite

Critical Insight:

The swimmer needs to account for river flow both before and after course correction.

We need to find his position after 10 seconds, then calculate the new direction.

JEE Strategy: Break complex problems into stages. Solve each stage systematically.

Stage 1: Initial Swimming

To reach directly opposite point:

sinθ = vr/vs

θ = sin⁻¹(3/5) = 36.87°

But Problem Says:

He doesn't swim at this angle initially

We don't know his initial direction

After 10s, he realizes he's off course

Assumption for Solution:

Let's assume he swam straight across initially (perpendicular to bank)

This is a common mistake swimmers make

Position After 10 Seconds

If swimming straight across:

Velocity component across river: 5 m/s

Velocity component downstream: 3 m/s

Distance across: 5 × 10 = 50 m

Distance downstream: 3 × 10 = 30 m

Position Vector:

r = 50î + 30ĵ

50 m across, 30 m downstream

Remaining distance across: 70 m

Stage 2: Course Correction

New Situation:

Remaining width: 70 m

Current position: 30 m downstream

Need to cancel downstream drift

Time Analysis:

Let t be time for second stage

Across component: vscosα × t = 70

Downstream component: vssinα × t - vrt = -30

Equations:

5cosα × t = 70

5sinα × t - 3t = -30

Solving the Equations

From first equation:

t = 70/(5cosα) = 14/cosα

Substitute in second equation:

5sinα × (14/cosα) - 3 × (14/cosα) = -30

70tanα - 42/cosα = -30

JEE Strategy: When dealing with trigonometric equations, try to express in terms of sin and cos or use substitution.

Trigonometric Solution

Our Equation:

70tanα - 42/cosα = -30

Multiply by cosα:

70sinα - 42 = -30cosα

70sinα + 30cosα = 42

Divide by 10:

7sinα + 3cosα = 4.2

Solving the Trigonometric Equation

Equation:

7sinα + 3cosα = 4.2

Use Rsin(α+β) method:

R = √(7² + 3²) = √58 ≈ 7.616

β = tan⁻¹(3/7) ≈ 23.2°

7.616 sin(α + 23.2°) = 4.2

Solve for α:

sin(α + 23.2°) = 4.2/7.616 ≈ 0.551

α + 23.2° = sin⁻¹(0.551) ≈ 33.4°

α ≈ 33.4° - 23.2° = 10.2°

Finding Time and Total Time

Time for Stage 2:

t = 14/cosα = 14/cos(10.2°)

cos(10.2°) ≈ 0.984

t ≈ 14/0.984 ≈ 14.23 s

Total Time:

Stage 1: 10 s

Stage 2: 14.23 s

Total: 24.23 s

Answer:

Total time ≈ 24.23 seconds

Angle for correction ≈ 10.2° upstream

Alternative Approach

Vector Method:

We can solve using relative velocity vectors

This is often faster for JEE problems

Position Vector:

r = 70î - 30ĵ

(relative to target point B)

Relative Velocity:

vsb = vswimmer - vriver

= 5(cosα î + sinα ĵ) - 3î

JEE Insight: Multiple approaches to solve the same problem demonstrate deep understanding of physics concepts.

Key Learnings

Conceptual Takeaways:

• River problems require vector analysis

• Course correction problems are common in JEE

• Break complex motion into stages

• Trigonometric equations often appear

Problem-Solving Strategy:

1. Understand the scenario

2. Break into stages

3. Write equations for each stage

4. Solve systematically

JEE Success Tip: Practice different types of river problems - they test your understanding of vectors and relative motion thoroughly.
