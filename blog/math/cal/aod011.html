Problem 1: Inverted Cone Filling with Water

Water is poured into an inverted conical tank at 2 m¬≥/min. The tank has height 12 m and top radius 6 m. How fast is the water level rising when the water is 4 m deep?

Solution Approach:

Step 1: Volume of cone: $V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$

Step 2: Similar triangles: $\frac{r}{h} = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow r = \frac{h}{2}$

Step 3: Substitute: $V = \frac{1}{3}\pi (\frac{h}{2})^2 h = \frac{\pi}{12}h^3$

Step 4: Differentiate: $\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{\pi}{12} \cdot 3h^2 \frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{\pi}{4}h^2 \frac{dh}{dt}$

Step 5: Plug values: $2 = \frac{\pi}{4}(4)^2 \frac{dh}{dt}$

Step 6: Solve: $\frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{2}{\frac{\pi}{4} \cdot 16} = \frac{1}{2\pi}$ m/min

JEE Main 2022 Hard

Problem 2: Spherical Balloon Inflation

Air is pumped into a spherical balloon at 10 cm¬≥/s. How fast is the surface area increasing when the radius is 5 cm?

Solution Approach:

Step 1: Volume: $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$, Surface Area: $S = 4\pi r^2$

Step 2: Differentiate volume: $\frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2 \frac{dr}{dt}$

Step 3: Plug values: $10 = 4\pi (5)^2 \frac{dr}{dt} \Rightarrow \frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{10}{100\pi} = \frac{1}{10\pi}$ cm/s

Step 4: Differentiate surface area: $\frac{dS}{dt} = 8\pi r \frac{dr}{dt}$

Step 5: Substitute: $\frac{dS}{dt} = 8\pi (5) \cdot \frac{1}{10\pi} = 4$ cm¬≤/s

JEE Main 2021 Medium

Problem 3: Cylindrical Tank Draining

A cylindrical tank of radius 3 m is being drained at 5 m¬≥/min. How fast is the height of water decreasing when the water is 8 m deep?

Solution Approach:

Step 1: Volume: $V = \pi r^2 h = \pi (3)^2 h = 9\pi h$

Step 2: Differentiate: $\frac{dV}{dt} = 9\pi \frac{dh}{dt}$

Step 3: Water is draining: $\frac{dV}{dt} = -5$ m¬≥/min

Step 4: Plug values: $-5 = 9\pi \frac{dh}{dt}$

Step 5: Solve: $\frac{dh}{dt} = -\frac{5}{9\pi}$ m/min

Step 6: Height is decreasing at $\frac{5}{9\pi}$ m/min

üöÄ Problem-Solving Strategy

Step-by-Step Approach:

  1. Draw diagram and label all variables
  2. Write given rates and what to find
  3. Establish relationship between variables
  4. Differentiate with respect to time
  5. Substitute values at specific instant
  6. Solve for the unknown rate

Common Relationships:

  • Cones: $r/h$ ratio constant (similar triangles)
  • Spheres: All dimensions scale together
  • Cylinders: Radius constant, height varies
  • Chain Rule: $\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{dV}{dr} \cdot \frac{dr}{dt}$
