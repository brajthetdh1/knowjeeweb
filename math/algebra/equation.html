A polynomial is an algebraic expression consisting of variables and coefficients, combined using addition, subtraction, and multiplication. Example: $x^2 + 3x + 2$.

Definition

A polynomial in variable $x$ of degree $n$ is written as: $ P(x) = a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + \dots + a_1 x + a_0 $ , where $a_n \neq 0$.

Example: Solve a quadratic polynomial

Solve $x^2 + 3x + 2 = 0$.

Factorize: x^2 + 3x + 2 = (x+1)(x+2)

So the roots are $x = -1, -2$.

Properties

  • Degree of a polynomial is the highest power of the variable.
  • Sum or difference of two polynomials is a polynomial.
  • Product of two polynomials is a polynomial.

Fun Fact

Polynomials are used to approximate complex functions and model physical phenomena in engineering and physics.
