What is Binomial Distribution?

The Binomial Distribution is one of the most important probability distributions in statistics and mathematics. It models experiments with exactly two possible outcomes - commonly called "success" and "failure".

1. Bernoulli Trials - The Foundation

Bernoulli Trial Definition

A Bernoulli Trial is a random experiment with exactly two possible outcomes. For a sequence of trials to be binomial, they must satisfy:

‚ë†
Fixed Trials
Number of trials (n) is fixed
‚ë°
Independent
Trials don't affect each other
‚ë¢
Constant Probability
Same success probability (p) each trial

Example: Coin Toss Experiment

Situation: Toss a fair coin 10 times

  • Fixed trials: n = 10 tosses
  • Independent: One toss doesn't affect another
  • Constant probability: p = 0.5 for heads each time

‚úì This is a binomial experiment!

2. The Binomial Probability Formula

The Core Formula

Binomial Probability Mass Function

$$ P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} $$
P(X = k)
Probability of exactly k successes
$\binom{n}{k}$
Combinations: $\frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}$
$p^k$
Probability of k successes
$(1-p)^{n-k}$
Probability of (n-k) failures

Example Calculation

Problem: What's the probability of getting exactly 7 heads in 10 coin tosses?

Given: n = 10, k = 7, p = 0.5

Calculation:

$P(X = 7) = \binom{10}{7} (0.5)^7 (0.5)^{3}$

$= 120 \times (0.5)^{10}$

$= 120 \times 0.0009765625$

$= 0.1171875$

Answer: Approximately 11.72%

4. Cumulative Probabilities

Beyond Single Probabilities

In real problems, we often need probabilities for ranges of values, not just single points.

At Most k Successes

$P(X \leq k) = \sum_{i=0}^{k} \binom{n}{i} p^i (1-p)^{n-i}$

Example: Probability of at most 3 heads in 5 tosses

At Least k Successes

$P(X \geq k) = \sum_{i=k}^{n} \binom{n}{i} p^i (1-p)^{n-i}$

Example: Probability of at least 7 correct in 10 questions

üéØ JEE Problem Strategy

Remember: $P(X \geq k) = 1 - P(X \leq k-1)$

This conversion often simplifies calculations in JEE problems!

Problem 1: Basic Calculation

A fair die is rolled 8 times. What is the probability of getting exactly 3 sixes?

Solution: n=8, k=3, p=1/6

$P(X=3) = \binom{8}{3} (\frac{1}{6})^3 (\frac{5}{6})^5 = 56 \times 0.00463 \times 0.40188 ‚âà 0.1042$

6. Common Mistakes to Avoid

JEE Pitfalls in Binomial Problems

‚ùå
Confusing with other distributions

Binomial is for fixed n, independent trials. Don't use it for problems without fixed trials!

‚ùå
Forgetting the combination term

$\binom{n}{k}$ accounts for different arrangements of successes

‚ùå
Mishandling "at least" and "at most"

Remember: P(X‚â•k) = 1 - P(X‚â§k-1)

‚ùå
Using wrong probability values

Ensure p represents success probability, not failure probability
