Question 1

Medium

If $\vec{a} = 2\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} - \hat{k}$ and $\vec{b} = \hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}$, find $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}$ and $|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}|$

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate dot product

$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (2)(1) + (3)(-2) + (-1)(3)$

$= 2 - 6 - 3 = -7$

Step 2: Calculate cross product

$\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 2 & 3 & -1 \\ 1 & -2 & 3 \end{vmatrix}$

$= \hat{i}(9-2) - \hat{j}(6+1) + \hat{k}(-4-3)$

$= 7\hat{i} - 7\hat{j} - 7\hat{k}$

Step 3: Find magnitude

$|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = \sqrt{7^2 + (-7)^2 + (-7)^2}$

$= \sqrt{49 + 49 + 49} = \sqrt{147} = 7\sqrt{3}$

Answer: $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = -7$, $|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = 7\sqrt{3}$

Question 2

Hard

Find the shortest distance between the lines:

$\vec{r} = (\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}) + \lambda(2\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} + 4\hat{k})$

$\vec{r} = (2\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} + 5\hat{k}) + \mu(3\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} + 5\hat{k})$

Solution:

Step 1: Identify components

Line 1: $\vec{a_1} = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}$, $\vec{b_1} = 2\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} + 4\hat{k}$

Line 2: $\vec{a_2} = 2\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} + 5\hat{k}$, $\vec{b_2} = 3\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} + 5\hat{k}$

Step 2: Formula for shortest distance

$d = \frac{|(\vec{a_2} - \vec{a_1}) \cdot (\vec{b_1} \times \vec{b_2})|}{|\vec{b_1} \times \vec{b_2}|}$

Step 3: Calculate $\vec{a_2} - \vec{a_1}$

$\vec{a_2} - \vec{a_1} = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 2\hat{k}$

Step 4: Calculate $\vec{b_1} \times \vec{b_2}$

$\vec{b_1} \times \vec{b_2} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 2 & 3 & 4 \\ 3 & 4 & 5 \end{vmatrix}$

$= \hat{i}(15-16) - \hat{j}(10-12) + \hat{k}(8-9)$

$= -\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} - \hat{k}$

Step 5: Calculate dot product and magnitude

$(\vec{a_2} - \vec{a_1}) \cdot (\vec{b_1} \times \vec{b_2}) = (1)(-1) + (2)(2) + (2)(-1) = -1 + 4 - 2 = 1$

$|\vec{b_1} \times \vec{b_2}| = \sqrt{1 + 4 + 1} = \sqrt{6}$

Step 6: Find distance

$d = \frac{|1|}{\sqrt{6}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{6}$

Answer: $\frac{\sqrt{6}}{6}$ units

Question 3

Medium

Find the equation of the plane passing through the point $(2, 3, -4)$ and perpendicular to the vector $\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} - 3\hat{k}$

Solution:

Step 1: General equation of plane

$a(x-x_1) + b(y-y_1) + c(z-z_1) = 0$

Where $(a, b, c)$ is normal vector and $(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ is a point on plane

Step 2: Identify values

Point: $(2, 3, -4)$

Normal vector: $\vec{n} = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} - 3\hat{k}$

So, $a = 1, b = 2, c = -3$

Step 3: Substitute

$1(x-2) + 2(y-3) + (-3)(z-(-4)) = 0$

$x - 2 + 2y - 6 - 3z - 12 = 0$

$x + 2y - 3z - 20 = 0$

Answer: $x + 2y - 3z = 20$

Question 4

Hard

If $\vec{a}$, $\vec{b}$, $\vec{c}$ are unit vectors such that $\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} = \vec{0}$, find the value of $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} + \vec{b} \cdot \vec{c} + \vec{c} \cdot \vec{a}$

Solution:

Step 1: Given conditions

$|\vec{a}| = |\vec{b}| = |\vec{c}| = 1$ (unit vectors)

$\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} = \vec{0}$

Step 2: Take dot product with itself

$(\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}) \cdot (\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}) = 0$

Step 3: Expand

$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{a} + \vec{b} \cdot \vec{b} + \vec{c} \cdot \vec{c} + 2\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} + 2\vec{b} \cdot \vec{c} + 2\vec{c} \cdot \vec{a} = 0$

Step 4: Use unit vector property

$|\vec{a}|^2 = 1$, $|\vec{b}|^2 = 1$, $|\vec{c}|^2 = 1$

$1 + 1 + 1 + 2(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} + \vec{b} \cdot \vec{c} + \vec{c} \cdot \vec{a}) = 0$

Step 5: Solve

$3 + 2(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} + \vec{b} \cdot \vec{c} + \vec{c} \cdot \vec{a}) = 0$

$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} + \vec{b} \cdot \vec{c} + \vec{c} \cdot \vec{a} = -\frac{3}{2}$

Answer: $-\frac{3}{2}$

Question 5

Medium

Find the angle between the vectors $\vec{a} = \hat{i} + \hat{j} + \hat{k}$ and $\vec{b} = \hat{i} - \hat{j}$

Solution:

Step 1: Formula for angle between vectors

$\cos\theta = \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|}$

Step 2: Calculate dot product

$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (1)(1) + (1)(-1) + (1)(0) = 1 - 1 + 0 = 0$

Step 3: Calculate magnitudes

$|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{1^2 + 1^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{3}$

$|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{1^2 + (-1)^2 + 0^2} = \sqrt{2}$

Step 4: Find angle

$\cos\theta = \frac{0}{\sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{2}} = 0$

$\theta = \cos^{-1}(0) = 90°$ or $\frac{\pi}{2}$

Answer: $90°$ or $\frac{\pi}{2}$ radians (perpendicular vectors)

Question 6

Hard

Find the distance of the point $(1, 2, 3)$ from the plane $x + 2y + 2z = 5$

Solution:

Step 1: Formula for distance from point to plane

$d = \frac{|ax_1 + by_1 + cz_1 - d|}{\sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}}$

For plane $ax + by + cz = d$ and point $(x_1, y_1, z_1)$

Step 2: Identify values

Plane: $x + 2y + 2z = 5$

$a = 1, b = 2, c = 2, d = 5$

Point: $(1, 2, 3)$

Step 3: Substitute in formula

$d = \frac{|(1)(1) + (2)(2) + (2)(3) - 5|}{\sqrt{1^2 + 2^2 + 2^2}}$

$= \frac{|1 + 4 + 6 - 5|}{\sqrt{1 + 4 + 4}}$

$= \frac{|6|}{\sqrt{9}}$

$= \frac{6}{3} = 2$

Answer: 2 units

Question 7

Hard

Find the vector equation of the line passing through $(1, 2, 3)$ and parallel to the line $\frac{x-2}{3} = \frac{y+1}{2} = \frac{z-4}{5}$

Solution:

Step 1: Vector equation of a line

$\vec{r} = \vec{a} + \lambda\vec{b}$

Where $\vec{a}$ is position vector of a point and $\vec{b}$ is direction vector

Step 2: Find direction vector

Given line: $\frac{x-2}{3} = \frac{y+1}{2} = \frac{z-4}{5}$

Direction ratios: $(3, 2, 5)$

So, $\vec{b} = 3\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 5\hat{k}$

Step 3: Position vector of given point

Point: $(1, 2, 3)$

$\vec{a} = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}$

Step 4: Write equation

$\vec{r} = (\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}) + \lambda(3\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 5\hat{k})$

Answer: $\vec{r} = (\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}) + \lambda(3\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 5\hat{k})$
