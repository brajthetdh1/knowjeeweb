What is Energy?

Energy is the capacity to do work.

It's a scalar quantity with magnitude only.

SI Unit:

Joule (J)

1 J = 1 N·m = 1 kg·m²/s²

JEE Significance: Fundamental concept in mechanics, thermodynamics, and modern physics.

Key aspects:

Conserved

Transformable

Scalar

Types of Energy

Kinetic Energy

✓ Energy of motion

✓ K = ½mv²

✓ Always positive

✓ Frame dependent

Potential Energy

✓ Stored energy

✓ Position dependent

✓ Can be negative

✓ Conservative forces

JEE Memory Tip: Kinetic energy depends on frame, Potential energy depends on reference point.

Kinetic Energy Formulas

Linear Motion

K = ½mv²

m = mass, v = speed

Rotational Motion

K = ½Iω²

I = moment of inertia, ω = angular velocity

Work-Energy Theorem: Net work done = Change in kinetic energy

W_net = ΔK = K_final - K_initial

Potential Energy Types

Gravitational PE

U = mgh

Near Earth's surface

Spring PE

U = ½kx²

Elastic potential energy

Electric PE

U = kq₁q₂/r

Coulomb's law

Gravitational PE

U = -GMm/r

Universal gravitation

Conservation of Energy

Law of Conservation of Energy

K_initial + U_initial = K_final + U_final

Total mechanical energy remains constant

Valid for conservative forces only

Conservative Forces

✓ Work independent of path

✓ Mechanical energy conserved

✓ Examples: Gravity, Spring

Non-conservative Forces

✓ Work depends on path

✓ Mechanical energy not conserved

✓ Examples: Friction, Air resistance

JEE Example Problem 1

JEE Main 2022 Question:

A ball of mass 2kg is dropped from 20m height. Find its kinetic energy just before hitting the ground.

Solution:

Method 1: Using Conservation of Energy

Initial energy: U = mgh = 2×9.8×20 = 392J

Final energy: K = ½mv²

K_final = U_initial = 392J

Method 2: Using Kinematics

v² = u² + 2gh = 0 + 2×9.8×20 = 392

K = ½×2×392 = 392J

JEE Example Problem 2

JEE Advanced 2020 Question:

A block of mass m compresses a spring by x and is released. Find maximum height reached if it leaves the spring vertically.

Solution:

Step 1: Spring energy converts to kinetic energy

½kx² = ½mv²

v = √(kx²/m)

Step 2: Kinetic energy converts to potential energy

½mv² = mgh

½m(kx²/m) = mgh

h = kx²/(2mg)

Power

Power = Rate of doing work

P = W/t = F·v

SI Unit: Watt (W) = 1 J/s

Average Power

P_avg = Total work / Total time

Use for constant force

Instantaneous Power

P_inst = dW/dt = F·v

Use for variable force
