Why Argand Plane is Crucial for JEE

The Argand Plane transforms abstract complex numbers into visual geometry, making problem-solving intuitive. This approach helps in:

  • Geometric interpretation of complex number operations
  • Visualizing modulus and argument as distance and angle
  • Solving locus problems geometrically
  • Understanding transformations like rotation and dilation
  • Scoring 4-8 marks in every JEE paper
Concept 1 Basic

The Argand Plane Basics

Representing $z = x + iy$ as point $(x, y)$ in coordinate plane

Argand Plane Representation

X-axis: Real axis

Y-axis: Imaginary axis

$z = x + iy \rightarrow (x, y)$

Key Components:

Real Part (x): Coordinate on horizontal axis

Imaginary Part (y): Coordinate on vertical axis

Modulus ($|z|$): Distance from origin: $\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$

Argument ($\theta$): Angle with positive real axis: $\tan^{-1}(\frac{y}{x})$

Concept 2 Intermediate

Geometric Interpretation of Operations

How complex number operations translate to geometric transformations

Addition:

Vector addition using parallelogram law

$z_1 + z_2$: Complete the parallelogram with $z_1$ and $z_2$

Multiplication:

Rotation and scaling

$z_1 \cdot z_2$: Multiply moduli, add arguments

$|z_1 \cdot z_2| = |z_1||z_2|$, $\arg(z_1 \cdot z_2) = \arg(z_1) + \arg(z_2)$

Conjugate:

Reflection about real axis

$\overline{z}$: Mirror image across x-axis

Concept 3 Advanced

Locus Problems in Argand Plane

Geometric representation of complex equations

Common Locus Patterns:

$|z - z_1| = r$

Circle with center $z_1$ and radius $r$

$|z - z_1| = |z - z_2|$

Perpendicular bisector of segment joining $z_1$ and $z_2$

$\arg(z - z_1) = \theta$

Ray from $z_1$ making angle $\theta$ with real axis

$|z - z_1| + |z - z_2| = 2a$

Ellipse with foci at $z_1$ and $z_2$

JEE Advanced Example:

Find locus of $z$ if $\arg\left(\frac{z-1}{z+1}\right) = \frac{\pi}{4}$

Solution: Arc of circle passing through (1,0) and (-1,0) with angle subtended = $\frac{\pi}{2}$
